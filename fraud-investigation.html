Fraud Investigation Case Study

Objective

This analysis focuses on identifying patterns associated with fraudulent financial transactions by examining transaction-level data. The goal is to uncover behavioral indicators that can support fraud detection and improve risk monitoring strategies.

Distribution of transaction patterns across fraud vs non-fraud cases

Problem

Financial transaction systems generate large volumes of data, making it difficult to detect fraudulent activity in real time. This project focuses on identifying behavioral patterns and risk indicators that differentiate fraudulent transactions from legitimate ones.

Approach

The analysis involved data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and feature engineering to understand transaction behavior. Key variables such as transaction amount, frequency, and account activity were analyzed to identify anomalies and patterns associated with fraud.

Key Goals

  • Identify behavioral patterns linked to fraudulent transactions
  • Compare characteristics of fraudulent vs legitimate transactions
  • Highlight high-risk transaction behaviors
  • Support development of risk monitoring strategies

Tools Used

  • Python – Data analysis and exploration
  • Pandas & NumPy – Data manipulation
  • Matplotlib / Seaborn – Data visualization

Key Insights

  • Fraudulent transactions showed distinct behavioral patterns compared to legitimate transactions.
  • Fraud is primarily driven by behavioural anomalies rather than demographic characteristics.
  • Location mismatch and foreign transactions are the strongest contextual indicators of fraud.
  • Fraud probability increases significantly when transaction velocity becomes unusually high.
  • Transactions originating from low device trust scores show a much higher likelihood of fraud.
  • Patterns observed suggest opportunities for rule-based monitoring and early fraud detection.

Business Impact

The findings from this analysis can support financial institutions in improving fraud detection systems by identifying high-risk transaction behaviors earlier. These insights can be used to enhance transaction monitoring rules, reduce fraud losses, and improve overall risk management processes. Ways in which this investigation can support Fraud & Risk teams

  • Improving fraud monitoring efficiency by reducing unnceccessary manual reviews
  • Strengthening risk based transactional reviews
  • Supporting the design of rule based fraud detection frameworks
